Bucketed Risk Detail
Cash holdings are treated as long positions in Bucket 1, netting against same-currency near-term payables. Forward exposures are bucketed by settlement date. Within each bucket, positions in the same currency are netted (natural hedge benefit), then the remaining net positions across currencies are combined using a covariance matrix (diversification benefit). Each bucket VaR uses its own time horizon T and cannot be summed across buckets.
ⓘ Cash positions appear in Bucket 1 as synthetic receivables and net against same-currency near-term obligations. No combined total across buckets — each bucket uses a different time horizon T. Each bucket VaR is meaningful and actionable within its own settlement window.

Model Notes & Limitations
Model — Parametric (Delta-Normal) VaR with Drift  ·  V3  ·  Built by Aayan Vatsa, Finmo (June 2026)

VaR Formula: VaR = E × (Z_α × σ_T − μ_T) for long positions (cash holdings, receivables) — fear is FCY depreciation, so loss is at the left tail of the return distribution. VaR = E × (Z_α × σ_T + μ_T) for short positions (payables) — fear is FCY appreciation, so loss is at the right tail. σ_T = σ_annual × √(T/252) scales volatility to horizon T; μ_T = μ_daily × T scales drift linearly. Both E and σ are in base currency terms. Z_α = norm.ppf(confidence), e.g. 1.645 at 95%. A negative raw VaR (drift exceeds downside vol) is floored to zero for display — this does NOT mean zero risk; a sudden trend reversal would not be captured.

Confidence level: 95% VaR means there is a 5% chance of losing more than the stated amount over the stated horizon under normal market conditions. It does not bound worst-case losses — the 5% tail can contain arbitrarily large losses, especially for EM currencies.

Output sections: Cash Book Risk — standalone VaR on cash at user-specified T, covariance-adjusted across currencies. Bucketed Risk Detail — cash routed into Bucket 1, forwards bucketed by settlement date; within-currency netting then cross-currency covariance applied per bucket; bucket VaRs use midpoint T and cannot be summed across buckets. Consolidated Portfolio VaR — exact cross-horizon formula: Σ[i,j] = ρ[i,j] × σᵢ_daily × σⱼ_daily × min(Tᵢ, Tⱼ), where min(Tᵢ, Tⱼ) is derived from the random walk assumption (only overlapping days survive when expanding the covariance of two cumulative return series). Same-currency pairs use ρ=1; natural hedging falls out automatically through signed exposures — no explicit netting step.

Component CFaR (dashboard bars): Each bar shows a currency's marginal contribution to total period risk via the Euler decomposition: ComponentVaR_i = (sᵢ × (Σs)ᵢ / σ_p) × Z − sᵢ × μᵢ × Tᵢ. Components sum exactly to Period VaR. A currency with large notional can show low Component CFaR if its positions hedge others in the portfolio — this is correct, not an error. For standalone risk per position see the Bucketed Risk Detail section.

Gross Standalone Risk: Sum of all individual position VaRs with no netting and no cross-currency diversification (perfect correlation assumed). Includes cash at a fixed 10-trading-day horizon (identical to what Consolidated VaR uses for cash — independent of the Cash VaR Horizon dropdown above) and forwards at bucket-midpoint T. Compared against Consolidated Portfolio VaR to produce the Risk Reduction percentage. The one remaining approximation is on the forwards side: bucket-midpoint T in the gross baseline vs each position's actual settlement T in Consolidated VaR, which slightly overstates the gross baseline and makes risk reduction marginally approximate.

Market data: Daily closing prices from Yahoo Finance (yfinance) over the user-selected lookback period (default 1 year). Returns are computed as daily percentage changes. Annualised vol = σ_daily × √252; drift = mean daily return. Thinly traded or exotic pairs (e.g. MYR/SGD, THB/SGD) are synthesised via USD cross-rates: spot(FCY/BCY) = spot(FCY/USD) × spot(USD/BCY); returns are summed (exact for log-returns, close approximation for percentage returns).

Known limitations and suggestions for future development:
Limitation What it means Suggested improvement
Normality assumption Returns assumed normally distributed. Understates tail risk for crashing EM currencies (TRY, ARS, IDR) with fat tails and negative skew. Monte Carlo simulation with Student's t distribution.
Linear instruments only Exact for cash and FX forwards. Cannot price the asymmetric payoff of FX options or any exotic structures. Monte Carlo simulation for portfolios with optionality.
Unofficial data source yfinance scrapes Yahoo Finance and can break without notice, causing silent fetch failures for affected currencies. Licensed vendor: Bloomberg, Refinitiv, or FRED + CurrencyLayer.
No holiday calendar Trading days counted Mon–Fri only. Market holidays (SGX, NYSE, MAS, BNM) are ignored, slightly misaligning T for some pairs. pandas_market_calendars for market-specific business day counting.
Bucket midpoint T Bucketed VaR uses each bucket's midpoint T rather than each exposure's exact settlement T, marginally overstating VaR for exposures early in a bucket. Consolidated VaR always uses exact T. Exposure-weighted average actual T per bucket instead of fixed midpoint.
Slider approximation Vol and spot sliders scale Component CFaRs without rerunning the covariance matrix. Period VaR during sliding is a conservative approximation; Portfolio VaR card is always exact. Lightweight /simulate endpoint that reruns covariance server-side on slider change.
Static correlations Correlation matrix estimated from the lookback window and assumed constant. Correlation breakdown during stress periods (2008, COVID) is not captured. DCC-GARCH dynamic correlations or a stressed correlation matrix overlay.
