Model — Full Parametric (Delta-Normal) VaR, V2

Spot Risk: Cash positions use a user-specified horizon T. σ_T = σ_annual × √(T/252); μ_T = μ_daily × T. VaR = E × (Z_α × σ_T − μ_T) for long (cash/receivable), VaR = E × (Z_α × σ_T + μ_T) for short (payable).

Forward Risk: Each exposure's horizon T = trading days from today to settlement date. Exposures are grouped into time buckets (0–1mo, 1–3mo, 3–6mo, 6–12mo, >12mo) and netted within each bucket/currency. VaR is computed on the net notional using the bucket midpoint T.

Natural Hedging Benefit: Within each bucket/currency group, gross VaR (sum of standalone VaRs at bucket T) minus net VaR (VaR on net notional). Both computed at the same bucket T so the difference is purely from netting. A positive benefit means offsetting positions reduced risk.

Limitations: Normality assumption; historical (backward-looking) volatility; simple sum across currencies within each bucket (assumes perfect correlation); natural hedging only within forward buckets (cash vs forward netting requires treasury policy assumptions and is left for V2.3+). Monte Carlo with Student's t for fat tails planned for V3.
